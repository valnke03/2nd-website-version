About

Background across cognitive science, philosophy, and data science, with an emphasis on rigorous methods and clear communication.

Bio

Valery Nkenguruke holds a Master of Arts in Cognitive Science (Lund University) and is currently completing a Magister in Computer/Data Science (Skövde University). Earlier training in theoretical philosophy and psychology supports strong conceptual analysis alongside empirical work.

Recent work includes an experimental thesis on how different verbalization protocols affect insight problem-solving, pilot studies using Real-time Speech Exchange to study speech monitoring and agency, and applied machine learning projects with careful evaluation design.

Research identity

Experimental cognition

Focus on problem representation, insight, and the interaction between language and cognitive processing.

Computational methods

Applied modeling and evaluation for behavioral and domain datasets, including time-aware testing and grouped validation.

Human–AI interaction

Interest in educational technology and interactive systems, including VR and language models as learning scaffolds.

Principles

Rigor

Strong baselines, transparent assumptions, and evaluation aligned with the scientific question.

Interpretability

Preference for explanations and error analyses that reveal what drives performance.

Reproducibility

Readable code, documented pipelines, and clear reporting of results, limitations, and next steps.
